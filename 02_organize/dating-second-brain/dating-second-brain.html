Where I May Not Be Seeing Clearly
These are recurring patterns in how I evaluate candidates. Not failures, just tendencies to watch.
I may place extra weight on early chemistry and intensity
After high-energy dates, I tend to feel more excited about a candidate than their overall compatibility warrants. The feeling is real, but it can crowd out quieter, more structural signals. Right now, this may be influencing how I see Mia relative to the others.
I may underrate how much day-to-day friction matters
Stability, routine compatibility, and communication quality don't create excitement on a first date, but they're what makes month 6 work. I've historically undercounted these in early evaluation.
I may overestimate my ability to absorb structural gaps
"I can manage this" often means I'm willing to carry the load, not that it's actually manageable. When I find myself thinking this about someone, it's worth asking what I'd be carrying and for how long. This pattern is currently active with Mia.
I may mistake social polish for deeper substance
Charisma, curiosity, and vibe make someone compelling to be around. But they're not the same as capability, groundedness, or communication quality. I should be careful not to let surface magnetism fill in the blanks where evidence is thin.
I may explain away friction when someone is exciting
When attraction is high, inconsistency becomes "just her vibe" and instability becomes "artist energy." The rationalizing language is worth noticing. It usually means I'm already invested and constructing a story that supports continuing.
I may undervalue people who don't create immediate spark
Stable, kind, capable candidates can feel "fine" early and "right" later. I've called Li Ming "one bubble away" and Jess "flat" based on very early data. Worth checking whether that's signal or just the absence of a dopamine spike.

What I'm Learning Over Time
Observations from the pattern of who I pursue, how I evaluate, and what actually works
I tend to invest fastest in whoever feels most alive
Across recent dating, my strongest initial excitement consistently tracks with high Attraction, Positive Energy, and Social Intelligence. These are Explorer-weighted traits. The candidates who feel most alive in unstructured settings get my attention first, even when their Operator fit is weaker.
Stability signals are invisible early
Groundedness, Capability, and Communication don't create chemistry on date 1-3. They create relief on month 3-6. Historically, I underweight these in the first 30 days and then wish I hadn't.
"I can manage it" is usually a warning, not a strength
When I think I can absorb someone's instability or fill in the structural gaps, the relationship tends to add cognitive load rather than reduce it. This thought pattern feels like confidence but functions as cost absorption.
My date environments are biased toward Explorer settings
Bars, events, and socially rich environments naturally favor high-energy, socially fluent candidates. Calmer, more grounded people may not shine in those contexts. If I only test in Explorer mode, my results will be skewed toward Explorer-fit candidates.
